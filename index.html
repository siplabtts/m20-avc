M2O-AVC: Many-to-One Token Based Accent and Voice Conversion

There's the hitch replied Thorpe rolling a cigarette
Speech Information Processing Lab, Indian Institute of Technology, Hyderabad

Abstract

We propose a novel reference-free accent and voice conversion (AVC) framework that achieves high-quality accent transfer with minimal training data while preserving source speaker linguistic content. Unlike neural machine translation or autoregressive native token-to-target token conversion methods followed by token-to-mel prediction, which risk content loss and model complexity, our approach uses deduplicated tokens to eliminate source speech rhythm and directly predicts target accented speech waveforms. Our framework leverages VITS2 (flow-based) architecture for token-to-speech waveform prediction, capable of converting diverse accents including Indian regional variants, Arabic, and Vietnamese accents to US English accented speech, and notably, the challenging reverse direction of converting Western accents (US and British) to Indian English accented speech—a rarely addressed scenario in accent conversion research. We achieve an accent similarity score of 4.6/5.0, significantly surpassing unconverted speech (1.5/5.0), representing a 3.1-point improvement.
